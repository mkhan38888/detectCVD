# Early Detection of Cardiovascular Disease Using AI

## What This Research Is About
Every year, 695,000 Americans die from cardiovascular disease. 
Many of these deaths could be prevented if warning signs were 
detected earlier. This research uses artificial intelligence 
to analyze heart rhythm data (ECG signals) and identify 
early signs of dangerous conditions like arrhythmia.

## The Problem I Am Solving
Current monitoring methods require patients to visit hospitals 
or wear bulky devices. My research focuses on building AI 
models small enough to run on everyday wearable devices like 
smartwatches, enabling continuous monitoring outside clinical 
settings — including in rural and underserved communities.

## Dataset
I am using the PTB-XL dataset — a collection of 21,799 
clinical ECG recordings from Leipzig Heart Centre, Germany, 
widely used in cardiovascular AI research.

## Current Progress
- Data loading and preprocessing pipeline complete
- Baseline deep learning model under development
- Target: arrhythmia classification at under 100ms latency
